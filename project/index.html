Flock of Cranes, 1767-1784

Ishida Yutei

A multitude of cranes, in white, black, gray, and red, traverse a gold-leaf background. At the center, a pair of cranes watches over their chicks, symbolizing marital fidelity and family happiness—a popular motif in wedding and dowry items.

The Battle of Pulo Aura, 1804

William Barnard

The Battle of Pulo Aura, fought on 15 February 1804 in the Strait of Malacca, was a significant naval engagement during the Napoleonic Wars. This battle saw the defeat of the French fleet, commanded by Rear-Admiral Charles-Alexandre Léon Durand, comte de Linois, by the British East India Company's fleet, led by Commodore Nathaniel Dance. The view is part of a key to a plate titled "Naval Engagement View of the Action between the China Fleet under the Command of Sir Nathaniel Dance, and a Squadron of French Ships of War commanded by Admiral Linois, in the China Sea, on the 15th of February 1804." This key was created by Sartorius, engraved by Barnard, and published by Andrews in 1804. The verso of the print includes a modern hand annotation: "XV/111.b CMB ’87 Jan," and it is cataloged under George III.
